# Asset Tracking Software

Asset tracking software helps teams monitor who has what, where it is, and how it moves — providing visibility, accountability, and control over shared equipment. Shelf is a free, [open-source](/solutions/open-source-asset-management) asset tracking platform built around QR codes, [custody logs](/features/custody), and [booking workflows](/features/bookings).

## What Is Asset Tracking?

Asset tracking is the process of monitoring physical equipment throughout its lifecycle: who has it, where it is located, when it moves, and what condition it is in. It applies to any organization that shares, lends, or distributes physical items — from cameras and laptops to tools and lab equipment.

Effective asset tracking answers three questions at any moment: **who has the item**, **where is it**, and **when is it due back**. Without a system in place, teams rely on memory, spreadsheets, and manual sign-out sheets that drift out of sync within days.

---

## How It Works — 3 Steps

**1. Label your assets.** Print QR code labels and attach them to every item you want to track. Labels are [customizable with your logo](/knowledge-base/how-to-print-custom-branded-qr-code-labels-with-shelf) and work with standard label printers.

**2. Scan to track.** Team members scan QR codes with any phone camera to check items in or out, update locations, and view booking availability. No app download required.

**3. See everything in real time.** Shelf's dashboard shows who has what, where it is, and when it is due back — across every team and location. Custody, location, and availability update automatically with every scan.

## Why Spreadsheets and Manual Tracking Fail

Organizations relying on manual methods hit the same problems:

- **Lost equipment with no responsibility trail**: Items disappear and no one knows who had them last. [CES Utility Solutions](/case-studies/ces-70k-recovery) lost a $70,000 drone kit before switching to Shelf — they recovered it using a QR label scan from a stranger who found it.
- **Spreadsheets that are outdated the moment they are saved**: Manual records require discipline from every team member. One missed entry and the entire sheet is unreliable.
- **Missing accessories**: Chargers, cables, lenses, and components get separated from their parent items. Without [kit-level tracking](/features/kits), teams waste time hunting for parts before every use.
- **Scheduling conflicts**: Multiple people assume the same equipment is available. Double bookings lead to last-minute scrambles, delayed shoots, and missed deadlines.
- **No audit trail**: When something is damaged or missing, there is no history showing who used it, when, or in what condition.
- **No visibility across locations**: Multi-site teams — field crews, campuses, satellite offices — cannot see what equipment exists at other facilities.

These problems compound. A single missing laptop is an inconvenience. A pattern of lost, double-booked, and unaccountable equipment costs thousands of dollars per year and erodes operational trust.

### QR Scanning — Identify Any Asset in Seconds

Every asset in Shelf gets a unique QR code. Scan it with any phone camera to instantly see the item's details, custody history, current location, and booking status. No app needed — just open the camera.

Shelf supports [custom branded QR labels](/knowledge-base/how-to-print-custom-branded-qr-code-labels-with-shelf) with your logo and colors, bulk label printing (compatible with DYMO, Brother, and Zebra printers), and [alternative barcode formats](/knowledge-base/alternative-barcodes) like Code128 and Data Matrix.

### Custody Tracking — Know Who Has What, Always

Shelf automatically records who is responsible for each asset and when responsibility changed. Every custody transfer — check-out, return, reassignment — is logged with timestamps and user details.

The custody chain is permanent and searchable. Need to know who had the projector last Thursday? Filter by asset and date range. Need a signed record? Shelf generates [PDF custody agreements](/knowledge-base/understanding-and-using-pdf-agreements-for-asset-custody-in-shelf) with e-signatures.

### Location Tracking — Track Equipment Across Sites

Organize assets by building, floor, room, job site, vehicle, or storage area. Shelf supports up to [12 levels of location hierarchy](/knowledge-base/sublocations-organize-locations-into-hierarchies), so you can model your real-world layout precisely.

When an asset moves, scan it to update the location instantly. Shelf can also capture GPS coordinates from the scanning device, so you always know where an item was last seen.

### Bookings — Prevent Scheduling Conflicts

Reserve equipment ahead of time with a [visual booking calendar](/features/calendar). Shelf shows real-time availability and prevents double-bookings automatically. Team members can browse by day, week, or month to find open slots.

Bookings integrate with custody: when someone picks up reserved equipment, a scan confirms the checkout and starts the custody clock. Overdue items are highlighted automatically.

### Kits — Bundle Items That Belong Together

Group a camera with its lenses, batteries, charger, and memory cards into a single kit. When the kit is checked out, all components move together. When it is checked back in, Shelf flags any missing items.

This eliminates the "accessories scattered across three departments" problem that plagues equipment-heavy organizations.

See: [Kits](/features/kits)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Shelf really free?

Yes. The [free plan](/pricing) includes unlimited assets, QR codes, location tracking, and basic custody features for one user. The Team plan adds unlimited users, booking calendars, PDF agreements, bulk actions, and more. There is also an Enterprise tier for SSO, SCIM, and multi-workspace setups.

### Does Shelf require a mobile app?

No. Shelf is a progressive web app (PWA) that runs in any modern browser. You can install it to your phone's home screen for an app-like experience, but there is nothing to download from an app store. QR scanning works through your device's camera.

### Can I import assets from a spreadsheet?

Yes. Shelf supports [CSV import](/knowledge-base/importing-assets-to-shelf-csv-guide) with flexible column mapping. You can import asset names, descriptions, categories, locations, custom field values, and more. Downloadable templates are available.

### How does Shelf compare to spreadsheets?

Spreadsheets require every team member to update the same file consistently. One missed entry breaks the system. Shelf automates the hard parts: custody is logged when someone scans a QR code, bookings prevent conflicts automatically, and location updates happen at scan time. There is no manual data entry to forget.

### Is Shelf open source?

Yes. Shelf is [open source](/solutions/open-source-asset-management) under the AGPL license and can be self-hosted with Docker. Most teams use the hosted version at app.shelf.nu, which requires no setup and includes automatic updates.

### What if someone finds a lost item with a QR label?

Shelf's "Found-via-Scan" workflow lets anyone — even without a Shelf account — scan a QR label and notify the owner. This is how [CES Utility Solutions](/case-studies/ces-70k-recovery) recovered a $70,000 drone kit that was found by a stranger at a job site.
