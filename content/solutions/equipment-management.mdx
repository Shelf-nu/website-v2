## What Is Equipment Management?

Equipment management is the practice of tracking physical equipment throughout its operational life: who has each item, where it is located, when it is available, and what condition it is in.

It covers the full lifecycle of shared equipment:

- **Inventory**: Knowing what equipment you own, where it is stored, and what condition it is in.
- **Scheduling**: Reserving equipment in advance so multiple teams can share limited resources without conflicts.
- **Check-in/check-out**: Recording who takes equipment, when, and when they return it.
- **Accountability**: Maintaining a verifiable record of who was responsible for each item at any point in time.
- **Maintenance awareness**: Tracking warranty dates, service intervals, and condition notes so equipment is kept in working order.

Without a system, teams default to spreadsheets, email threads, and memory. These methods break down as soon as the number of items, users, or locations grows beyond a handful.

## Common Equipment Management Challenges

Organizations managing shared equipment face predictable problems:

- **Equipment goes missing** and no one knows who had it last. [CES Utility Solutions](/case-studies/ces-70k-recovery) lost a $70,000 drone kit before adopting Shelf — recovered only because a stranger found and scanned the QR label.
- **Scheduling conflicts** happen when multiple teams assume the same equipment is available. [Fabel Film](/case-studies/fabel-film-double-bookings) described their pre-Shelf process as running on one person's memory — every shoot was a gamble.
- **Spreadsheets are unreliable** the moment more than one person is involved. [Arellano Associates](/case-studies/arellano-associates) called their previous system "Outlook chaos."
- **Accessories get separated** from parent items. A camera without its charger, a laptop without its power adapter, a toolkit missing a drill bit — every incomplete item is a delayed project.
- **Multi-site visibility is nonexistent**. Field crews, satellite offices, and campus departments cannot see what is available at other locations.
- **No audit trail** means disputes about damage or loss have no resolution.

Shelf replaces fragmented tools with a single platform designed around QR scanning and structured workflows.

### Track Every Item with QR Codes

Every piece of equipment gets a unique QR code. Scan it with any phone camera to view details, check the item in or out, update its location, or see its booking status. No app download required.

Labels are [customizable with your logo and colors](/knowledge-base/how-to-print-custom-branded-qr-code-labels-with-shelf), and print on DYMO, Brother, and Zebra printers. [Alternative barcode formats](/knowledge-base/alternative-barcodes) (Code128, Code39, Data Matrix) are also supported.

### Know Who Has What — Always

Shelf's custody system automatically records every equipment handoff. When someone checks out a laptop, the system logs who took it, when, and when it is due back. When they return it, a second scan closes the loop.

The custody trail is permanent and searchable. For high-value items, Shelf generates [PDF custody agreements](/knowledge-base/understanding-and-using-pdf-agreements-for-asset-custody-in-shelf) with e-signatures.

See: [Custody](/features/custody)

### Schedule Equipment Without Conflicts

A [visual booking calendar](/features/calendar) (daily, weekly, monthly views) lets team members [reserve equipment](/solutions/equipment-reservations) in advance. Shelf blocks overlapping reservations automatically. Overdue items are highlighted so the next user is not left waiting.

See: [Bookings](/features/bookings)

### Organize by Location

Track equipment across buildings, floors, rooms, job sites, vehicles, and storage areas — up to [12 levels of location hierarchy](/knowledge-base/sublocations-organize-locations-into-hierarchies). QR scans update locations instantly. GPS coordinates from the scanning device are captured automatically.

See: [Location Tracking](/features/location-tracking)

### Bundle Accessories with Kits

Group related items together: camera + lenses + charger + memory cards. When a kit is checked out, everything moves together. Missing items are flagged during check-in.

See: [Kits](/features/kits)

### Set Reminders for Maintenance

Track warranty expirations, calibration due dates, service windows, and license renewals with [custom fields](/knowledge-base/custom-field-types-in-shelf) and time-based reminders. Notifications go to one or multiple team members via email and in-app alerts.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Shelf free?

Yes. The [free plan](/pricing) includes unlimited assets, QR codes, location tracking, and basic custody features for one user. Team and Enterprise plans add unlimited users, booking calendars, PDF custody agreements, and SSO.

### What equipment can I track with Shelf?

Anything physical: cameras, laptops, tools, lab instruments, AV gear, vehicles, furniture, medical devices, safety equipment, drones, maker space tools. If it has a surface for a QR label, Shelf can track it.

### How does Shelf compare to a spreadsheet?

Spreadsheets depend on manual data entry by every team member, every time. One missed update and the data is unreliable. Shelf automates tracking: scan a QR code to check out, scan again to return. Custody, location, and availability update automatically with no manual entry.

### Can I track equipment across multiple locations?

Yes. Shelf supports [hierarchical locations](/knowledge-base/sublocations-organize-locations-into-hierarchies) with up to 12 levels of nesting. Equipment can move between locations via QR scan, and administrators see everything from a single dashboard.

### Does Shelf work offline?

Shelf requires an internet connection for real-time features like custody transfers and booking updates. However, QR codes themselves work offline — scanning opens the asset page when connectivity is restored.

### Can external contractors use the system?

Yes. Non-Registered Members (NRM) allow you to assign custody to people who do not have a Shelf account. This is useful for contractors, event staff, students, or visitors.
