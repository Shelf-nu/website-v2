# Multi-Location Asset Tracking

Multi-location asset tracking is the ability to manage equipment across multiple physical sites — offices, warehouses, campuses, job sites, vehicles — from a single system. Instead of separate spreadsheets per location or manual coordination between site managers, a multi-location system provides unified visibility: what is where, who has it, and whether it is available.

Shelf is a free, [open-source](/solutions/open-source-asset-management) asset tracking platform with native multi-location support. Organize sites into hierarchies up to 12 levels deep, transfer equipment between locations with a QR scan, and see real-time availability across every site from one dashboard. Used by 3,000+ teams managing 450,000+ assets.

---

## The Multi-Location Problem

Organizations with equipment spread across multiple sites face a specific set of challenges that single-location tracking tools and spreadsheets cannot solve:

### No Cross-Site Visibility

Each location maintains its own records — a spreadsheet, a whiteboard, a mental model held by one site manager. When the main office needs a projector and one is sitting idle at the satellite office, no one knows. Equipment gets re-purchased because the search radius stops at the building door.

### Transfer Chaos

Moving equipment between sites involves emails, phone calls, and hope. There is no structured way to document that a laptop left Building A and arrived at Building B. Items get lost in transit, arrive without documentation, or sit in a loading dock unclaimed.

### Inconsistent Processes

Different locations develop different tracking habits. The warehouse uses a clipboard. The office uses a shared spreadsheet. The field team uses text messages. Reporting across the organization requires manually combining incompatible data sources.

### Audit Complexity

When it is time to verify equipment counts — for insurance, compliance, or financial reporting — teams spend days reconciling records across sites. Physical audits require travel. Discrepancies are common because the data was never unified in the first place.

## How Shelf Handles Multi-Location Tracking

Shelf treats location as a first-class dimension of every asset, not an afterthought field in a spreadsheet.

### Hierarchical Location Structure

Organize your sites into a tree structure that mirrors your real world. Shelf supports [up to 12 levels of location hierarchy](/knowledge-base/sublocations-organize-locations-into-hierarchies):

**Region → City → Campus → Building → Floor → Room → Shelf**

Or for field operations:

**District → Job Site → Vehicle → Toolbox**

Each level is a real location in Shelf with its own inventory, QR code, and activity history. Add, rename, or restructure locations at any time without losing historical data.

### Cross-Site Transfers via QR Scan

When equipment moves between locations, a QR scan at the destination updates the asset record automatically. The transfer is timestamped, attributed to the person who scanned, and logged in the asset's activity history.

No forms, no emails, no manual spreadsheet updates. The receiving site scans the item and the system reflects reality.

---

### Real-Time Dashboard Across All Sites

A single dashboard shows equipment counts, availability, and status across every location. Filter by site to see what is at a specific warehouse. Filter by category to see all laptops across the organization. Filter by status to see what is checked out, available, or overdue — across every location simultaneously.

Site managers see their own location. Administrators see everything.

### Location-Aware Bookings

When a team member reserves equipment, they see availability by location. A camera kit available at the downtown office but checked out at the campus is visible in one view. Cross-site reservations are possible — book it at one location, pick it up there, return it at another.

This is different from shared-folder approaches where everyone sees everything and access control becomes the bottleneck.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can different locations have different administrators?

Yes. Shelf supports role-based access so you can assign site-level managers who oversee their own location's equipment while organization administrators maintain visibility across all sites.

### How do I transfer equipment between locations?

Scan the asset's QR code at the new location. Shelf updates the location record automatically, with a timestamped log of the transfer and who performed it. No forms or approvals required unless you configure them.

### Can I see all equipment across all locations in one view?

Yes. The asset dashboard shows your entire inventory with filters for location, category, status, and assignee. You can view everything at once or narrow down to a specific site.

### How many locations can I create?

There is no limit on the number of locations. You can create as many sites, sub-locations, and hierarchy levels as your organization needs, up to 12 levels deep.

### Does Shelf work offline for remote job sites?

Shelf requires an internet connection to sync data in real time. For remote sites with intermittent connectivity, team members can scan QR codes when they have signal — the records update as soon as the connection is available.

### Can I import my existing location data?

Yes. The [CSV import](/knowledge-base/importing-assets-to-shelf-csv-guide) supports a location column. Map your existing site names to Shelf locations during import, and assets will be assigned to the correct site automatically.
