## Methodology

A report is only as good as its methodology. Here is the full disclosure, including the risks we managed.

**Data sources.**
- **Anonymized telemetry** from production Shelf workspaces. No customer-identifying data. All aggregates require cohorts of at least 20 workspaces; smaller cohorts are not reported.
- **A survey of {{TODO: surveyResponses}} workspace admins**, distributed via email and in-app during {{TODO: month}} 2026.

**Time window.** The 12 months ending **April 30, 2026** for telemetry. Survey responses collected in {{TODO: month}} 2026.

**Sample.** Approximately **{{TODO: workspaces}}** workspaces, **{{TODO: assets}}** tracked assets, **{{TODO: countries}}** countries, plus the survey responses.

**Inclusion criteria for telemetry.** A workspace is included if all of the following hold:
- Workspace type is **Team** (Personal workspaces excluded).
- Workspace is **not disabled**.
- Owner user is **not soft-deleted**.
- Workspace has tracked **at least 10 assets** during the window.
- Workspace is **not on the internal allowlist** (Shelf staff, demo workspaces, support workspaces).
- Feature-gated stats restrict to feature-enabled workspaces; sub-cohort gets its own k-anonymity check.

**Risk disclosures.** This is what separates a research artifact from a marketing piece:

1. **Feature-adoption risk.** Several Shelf features have bounded adoption (the Audits add-on is paid; not every workspace uses bookings). Any stat that depends on a feature is computed only over the workspaces using that feature, and the published prose explicitly says so. We do not present feature-subset stats as "median workspace" claims.

2. **`Asset.valuation` coverage.** The valuation field is workspace-entered and partial. Approximately **{{TODO: pct_assets_with_valuation}}%** of tracked assets carry a valuation. Dollar figures are computed only over the assets with a valuation, then median-extrapolated per workspace. **The published dollar figures are conservative lower bounds** — they exclude assets without explicit valuation entirely. If overall coverage falls below 30%, the dollar headline is replaced with the percentage headline.

3. **Cohort-size enforcement.** Every aggregate requires k=20 workspaces. The extraction script returns `cohort_too_small` for any stat that falls below; those stats are omitted from the published report, not silently rounded down.

4. **Survey response rate.** The survey targeted n=200 admins. Actual responses: **{{TODO: surveyResponses}}**. If responses fell below n=50, the survey-derived stat would have been dropped entirely.

**Definitions.** Where a stat could be defined multiple ways, we picked the most conservative definition.

- **Idle asset (THE HEADLINE DEFINITION).** An asset with no activity events — scan, custody change, booking event, location update, or audit scan — in the prior 90 days at end of window. Assets created within the 90-day idle window are excluded (brand-new assets without history are not idle, they're just new).

- **Idle-asset dollar value (THE HEADLINE).** For each workspace: sum `Asset.valuation` over the workspace's idle assets where valuation is set. The published figure is the median of those per-workspace sums. Median rather than mean because the distribution is right-skewed.

- **Active custody.** An asset has a `Custody` row (current custodian) at the moment of measurement.

- **Late return.** A booking whose actual return time exceeded its scheduled `to` time, or a booking still in `ONGOING` / `OVERDUE` status after its scheduled `to`.

- **Recovery via Found-via-Scan.** A `Scan` event from an anonymous (non-Shelf-account) scanner whose associated asset was previously marked Missing or Idle. Sum `Asset.valuation` across all recovered assets in the window for a single platform-wide total.

- **Ghost asset (audit-subset definition).** An asset that (a) exists in the inventory, (b) was on the expected list of two or more consecutive `AuditAsset` rows with status `MISSING`, (c) had no scan activity between those audits anywhere on the platform. **This stat applies only to workspaces with the Audits add-on enabled who ran at least one audit in the window. The published value is the median of THAT subset, never presented as the platform median.**

**Reproducibility.** The extraction script is published in the open at [github.com/Shelf-nu/shelf.nu](https://github.com/Shelf-nu/shelf.nu) under `apps/webapp/scripts/state-of-em-2026/`. The script contains no customer data — only queries and aggregation logic. A separate `--probe` mode verifies feature adoption before stats are computed, so the methodology can be re-validated by anyone with database access.

**Limitations.** Self-hosted Shelf instances are not included (cloud-hosted only). "Idle" is per-asset-per-90-days, not a permanent property. The sample skews toward teams that have already opted into structured asset management — industry-wide idle rates are likely higher.

## The dollar value of idle equipment

For the median Shelf workspace, **{{TODO: $X,XXX}}** of physical equipment sits unused for 90 days or more at a stretch.

The definition is precise. An idle asset is an item that:
1. Is recorded in the workspace's asset inventory,
2. Has had no scan, custody change, booking event, location update, or audit scan in the prior 90 days,
3. Existed before the 90-day window opened (brand-new untouched assets are not idle, they're just new).

Idle is not lost. Idle is unused. A drill in a tool crib that hasn't been checked out in three months may be off-season. A laptop with no custody activity may belong to a long-term employee who doesn't trigger interaction events. The point is not that idle assets are missing — it's that aggregated across an inventory, **idle assets are dead capital**: equipment the organization has paid for, depreciates against, and insures, without using.

Why idle equipment matters financially:

- **Procurement budget waste.** If 15% of your existing laptop fleet hasn't been touched in 90 days, the next quarter's procurement request is overstated by exactly that amount. The dead-capital cost shows up as next quarter's order, not last quarter's loss.
- **Depreciation against unused assets.** Depreciation schedules don't care whether equipment is used. Dead capital still depreciates.
- **Insurance overpayment.** Insured fleet value should reflect what's working, not what exists. Idle equipment inflates premium.
- **Headcount-per-device ratios** suggest equipment exists that's effectively absent. IT teams over-provision; field teams under-utilize.

Unlike the loss-rate framing favoured by traditional industry research, idle-rate is observable, definable, and reproducible. Telemetry sees idleness more cleanly than it sees loss — a missing asset might be recovered next month; an idle asset is just there, doing nothing, costing money.

## The accountability gap

Only **{{TODO: pct_assets_with_active_custody}}%** of tracked assets have an active custodian assigned.

Custody is the operational definition of accountability. An asset with a current custodian is an asset that, if it goes missing, has a clear human at the start of the chain. An asset without a custodian is a shared-pool item — functionally a public resource.

The gap between platform custody coverage and 100% is the upstream cause of most downstream loss and idleness. Workspaces with above-median custody coverage have measurably lower idle rates and — in the subset of workspaces that audit — lower missing rates.

Among workspaces that use the bookings feature, **{{TODO: bk_pct_returned_late}}%** of reservations ended after their scheduled return time.

Ghost assets are the audit-detectable subset of the broader idle-and-missing equipment problem. They are valuable because they isolate the assets that have genuinely vanished from operational reality — not just been displaced for a week. **For the workspaces that audit, ghost-asset rate is the best available measurement of true loss**. For workspaces that don't audit, the equivalent loss is unmeasured and almost certainly higher.

## Limitations and the 2027 baseline

A few honest caveats:

- **The Shelf-customer sample skews toward teams already practising structured asset management.** Industry-wide idle and missing rates are almost certainly higher than what we observe — we see the well-instrumented organizations.
- **`Asset.valuation` coverage.** Dollar figures are conservative lower bounds; they exclude assets whose workspace did not enter a valuation. We disclose the coverage percentage in the methodology and use percentage-only stats as fallback for any number where valuation coverage was inadequate.
- **Self-hosted instances are not in the data.** Shelf is open source; self-hosted patterns may differ from cloud patterns. We can't observe them and don't try to claim otherwise.
- **One year of data is one year of data.** Year-over-year diffs come into play in the 2027 edition. The 2026 numbers are baselines without comparison points yet.
- **Feature-adoption variance.** The bookings, audits, and Found-via-Scan stats each speak to subsets of the cohort. We publish them with explicit qualification rather than pretending they describe the median workspace.
- **Survey N.** Approximately 200 responses is adequate for medians and reportable themes; it is not sufficient to break out reliably by industry. The 2027 survey will aim for n=500.

**The State of Equipment Management 2027** will publish in the same window next year, with year-over-year trend lines added to every stat in this report. If there is a specific cut you would like investigated, [tell us](/contact); the 2027 extraction queries can be planned against requests.
