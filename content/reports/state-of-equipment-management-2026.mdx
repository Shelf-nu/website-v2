## Methodology

A report is only as good as its methodology. Here is the full disclosure:

**Data sources.** Two:
- **Anonymized telemetry** from production Shelf workspaces. No customer-identifying data was used. All numbers are aggregates over cohorts of at least 20 workspaces; cohorts smaller than 20 are not reported.
- **A survey of {{TODO: surveyResponses}} workspace admins**, distributed via email and in-app during {{TODO: month}} 2026.

**Time window.** The 12 months ending **April 30, 2026** for telemetry. Survey responses were collected in {{TODO: month}} 2026.

**Sample.** Approximately **{{TODO: workspaces}}** workspaces, **{{TODO: assets}}** tracked assets, **{{TODO: countries}}** countries. Inclusion criteria below.

**Inclusion criteria for telemetry.** A workspace is included if all of the following hold:
- Workspace type is **Team** (Personal workspaces excluded — they are individual sandboxes).
- Workspace is **not disabled**.
- Owner user is **not soft-deleted**.
- Workspace has tracked **at least 10 assets** during the window.
- Workspace is **not on the internal allowlist** (Shelf staff, demo workspaces, support workspaces).
- Feature-gated stats (audits, bookings) restrict to feature-enabled workspaces; the sub-cohort gets its own k-anonymity check.

**Anonymization.** Numerical aggregates are rounded to one significant figure. Cohorts smaller than 20 are not reported. No customer names, workspace names, user names, asset titles, or location names appear in any aggregate. Quotes attributed to specific customers in this report are published with their explicit permission.

**Definitions.** Where a stat could be defined multiple ways, we picked the most conservative definition.
- **Ghost asset.** An asset marked Missing in two or more consecutive audits within the window AND with no scan activity (QR scan or audit scan) between those audits. Excludes assets that have been recovered, reassigned, or scanned anywhere on the platform.
- **Ghost-asset dollar value.** For each ghost asset, use the workspace-entered `Asset.valuation` field. **Coverage caveat:** approximately {{TODO: pct_assets_with_valuation}}% of tracked assets have a valuation set; ghost-asset dollar values are computed only over the assets with a valuation, then median-extrapolated per workspace. Ghost dollar figures are conservative — they exclude unvalued assets entirely.
- **Active custody.** An asset has a `Custody` row (current custodian) at the moment of measurement.
- **Idle asset.** An asset with no activity events (scan, custody change, booking, location update) in the prior 90 days at end of window.
- **Audit completion.** An `AuditSession` that reached `COMPLETED` status within the window.

**Survey methodology.** The survey was sent to admins of eligible workspaces who had been active in the previous 30 days. 5 questions, single-page form, no incentive offered. Response rate: {{TODO: response_pct}}%. The full questionnaire is published alongside the dataset.

**Reproducibility.** The extraction script that produced these telemetry aggregates is published in the open at [github.com/Shelf-nu/shelf.nu](https://github.com/Shelf-nu/shelf.nu) under `apps/webapp/scripts/state-of-em-2026/`. The script contains no customer data — only the queries and aggregation logic.

**Limitations.** Self-hosted Shelf instances are not included — the report reflects hosted-cloud usage only. "Missing" in an audit context is per-session, not a permanent property of the asset. The Asset.valuation field is workspace-entered and incomplete; dollar figures should be read as conservative lower bounds, not exhaustive totals. The sample skews toward teams that have already opted into a structured asset-management practice — the industry-wide ghost-asset rate is likely higher than what we observe.

## The dollar value of ghost assets

For the median Shelf workspace, **{{TODO: $X,XXX}}** of physical equipment exists only on paper.

The definition is precise. A ghost asset is an item that:
1. Is recorded in the workspace's asset inventory,
2. Was expected at a specific location during a physical audit,
3. Did not appear in two consecutive audits at that location,
4. Has had no scan activity — QR scan, audit scan, location update — between those audits anywhere on the platform.

That last clause matters. An asset can fail to appear at an audit because it moved without the location being updated; once it's scanned at the new location, it's no longer a ghost. The ghost-asset definition isolates the assets that have genuinely vanished from operational reality.

Across the platform, ghost assets account for **{{TODO: ds_ghost_asset_rate}}%** of audited inventory. The dollar value comes from multiplying that rate by the median `Asset.valuation` field per workspace. **{{TODO: pct_assets_with_valuation}}%** of assets carry a valuation, so the figure is a conservative lower bound — it excludes assets without explicit valuation entirely.

Why ghost assets matter:

- **Budgets.** A ghost asset still appears in inventory counts. "We have 850 laptops" overstates the fleet if 10% of those are ghosts; the IT team plans procurement accordingly and over-buys.
- **Depreciation.** Ghost assets continue to depreciate on the financial books, generating phantom expense and inflating the depreciation total that flows to the income statement.
- **Insurance.** A fleet's insured value should reflect what exists. Workspaces overstating fleet value pay higher premiums than necessary; understating it leaves them under-covered when something is actually lost.
- **Headcount-per-device ratios.** IT and operations teams that compute "laptops per employee" or "tools per crew" overstate coverage if ghosts are in the count. The deployed-fleet-to-real-fleet gap shows up later as employees waiting for equipment that records claim already exists.

Nothing in the industry has historically measured this directly. Vendor surveys ask administrators what they believe is missing; this report measures what telemetry actually finds missing.

## The accountability gap

Only **{{TODO: pct_assets_with_active_custody}}%** of tracked assets have an active custodian assigned at any moment.

Custody is the operational definition of accountability. An asset with a current custodian is an asset that, if it goes missing, has a chain that begins with a specific human. An asset without a custodian is a shared-pool item with no clear accountability — functionally a public resource. Both can be operationally appropriate; the difference matters when something is lost.

The gap between the platform's custody-coverage rate and 100% is the upstream cause of most ghost assets. Workspaces with above-median custody coverage have measurably lower missing rates at audit time.

## Most teams don't audit

Only **{{TODO: au_pct_workspaces_running_audits}}%** of Team-tier workspaces with the Audits add-on enabled ran at least one audit in the year. In the audits that did happen, **{{TODO: au_pct_audited_assets_missing}}%** of expected assets came up Missing on the first scan.

The Missing rate is the most operationally important behavioural number in this report. It says: of the items records claim are in a specific location, roughly one in every **{{TODO: 100/missing_rate}}** is not actually there at the moment of audit.

Three caveats:
- An asset can be Missing because it moved without an update, not because it was lost. The next scan in another location resolves it.
- Missing rates are concentrated in specific categories — small portable items, kit components.
- Workspaces that audit on a regular cadence have lower Missing rates over time. Audits surface the problem; the problem then gets fixed.

The meta-finding: audit cadence is the single best predictor of low ghost-asset rates. Quarterly audits work measurably better than annual ones.

## The idle-asset parallel

The median workspace also has **{{TODO: $X,XXX}}** of equipment idle — no activity in the prior 90 days.

Idle is not lost. Idle is unused. A drill in a tool crib that hasn't been checked out in three months may simply be off-season; a laptop with no custody activity may belong to a long-term employee who doesn't trigger events. Idle is a softer signal than ghost. But aggregated across an inventory, idle assets are the equivalent of dead capital — procurement budget spent on items the organization isn't using.

Ghost assets distort what the books say you have. Idle assets distort what you should buy next. Together, they're the unmeasured equipment overhead carried by the average organization.

## The recovery story

Across the platform, **{{TODO: $X,XXX,XXX}}** of equipment was recovered via Found-via-Scan in the year.

Found-via-Scan is Shelf's public recovery flow: anyone, even without a Shelf account, can scan a QR label on a found item and notify the owner. The flow exists because the most useful person to recover an asset is often a stranger — someone who picked up the camera left in a rideshare, the drill left at a job site, the laptop left in a conference room.

This is the positive flip side of the ghost-asset finding. The mechanism that prevents an asset from becoming a ghost is sometimes a member of the public scanning a printed label and clicking a notification. The permanent QR label is both a tracking mechanism and a recovery mechanism.

## Limitations and the 2027 baseline

No report of this kind is the last word. A few honest caveats:

- **The Shelf-customer sample skews toward teams already practising structured asset management.** Industry-wide ghost-asset rates are likely higher than what we observe.
- **Asset.valuation coverage.** The dollar figures are conservative lower bounds; they exclude assets whose workspace did not enter a valuation.
- **Self-hosted instances are not in the data.** Shelf is open source and self-hostable; self-hosted patterns may differ.
- **One year of data is one year of data.** Year-over-year diffs come into play in the 2027 edition. The 2026 numbers are the baseline.
- **Survey N is small.** {{TODO: surveyResponses}} responses is adequate for medians and reportable themes; it is not sufficient to break out by industry segment with confidence. The 2027 survey will aim for n=500.

The **State of Equipment Management 2027** will be published in the same window next year, with year-over-year trend lines added to every stat in this report.
