# Equipment Tracking for Engineering & Research Labs

Research facilities, engineering workshops, and R&D labs manage specialized instruments and shared tools that move between teams, benches, and buildings. Oscilloscopes, multimeters, testing rigs, calibration tools, and prototype equipment are expensive to replace and critical to keep calibrated and available.

Traditional tracking methods — shared spreadsheets, paper sign-out logs, or memory — break down when multiple teams share the same instruments across shifts and locations. Shelf provides fast, QR-first workflows that give lab managers clear visibility into who has what, where it is, and when it needs attention.

---

## Common Challenges in Labs & Engineering Facilities

Engineering and research teams face recurring equipment management problems:

- **Instruments disappearing between teams**: Shared oscilloscopes, analyzers, and testing tools end up on someone's bench with no record.
- **Missed calibration cycles**: Critical instruments go out of calibration because no one tracked the schedule.
- **No visibility across buildings**: Equipment moves between labs, cleanrooms, and workshops without anyone updating a central record.
- **Unclear responsibility**: When a shared instrument is damaged or misconfigured, there's no audit trail showing who used it last.
- **Duplicate purchases**: Teams buy new equipment because they can't find or locate existing inventory.
- **Compliance gaps**: ISO 17025 and GLP requirements demand documented equipment logs that spreadsheets can't reliably provide.
