# EZOfficeInventory Alternative

EZOfficeInventory (now rebranded as EZO) is an asset tracking and IT service management platform known for its integrations with help desk tools like Zendesk and Jira. It is built for IT departments managing laptops, monitors, and office peripherals, with maintenance scheduling and service desk workflows at its core. Teams exploring alternatives typically need simpler pricing, faster day-to-day equipment operations, and workflows that extend beyond IT—covering shared gear, production equipment, and field tools across multiple departments.

---

## Overview: EZOfficeInventory vs Shelf

EZOfficeInventory positions itself at the intersection of asset tracking and IT service management. Its Zendesk and Jira integrations let support agents see which assets are assigned to a user when resolving tickets, and its CMMS features help IT teams schedule preventive maintenance and track device lifecycles. Plans start at $35/month for 250 items, scaling by $15/month for each additional 250 items, with advanced features like service desk integrations and SSO locked to the Premium tier at $55/month.

Shelf focuses on the operational side of equipment management. Rather than building around ticketing systems, Shelf optimizes the physical workflows—scanning, checking out, booking, returning, and transferring custody. Shelf’s Team plan starts at $67/month with unlimited items and users included, so teams can grow without hitting item caps or calculating per-tier add-ons.

---

## Where Shelf Takes a Different Approach

### 1. QR-First Operations vs Dashboard-First Administration

EZOfficeInventory centers its workflows on a web dashboard where administrators manage assets, assign items, and resolve tickets. Users report that some common actions—like canceling a reservation—take several steps once you locate the right screen. Shelf puts the power at the point of interaction: staff scan a QR code on the equipment itself and complete check-out, return, or transfer on the spot in seconds. No dashboard navigation required.

### 2. Booking System Built for Shared Equipment

EZOfficeInventory includes reservations, but its primary model is assignment—one laptop assigned to one employee. Its reservation system is built around that paradigm. Shelf’s booking system is designed from the ground up for shared pools: multiple users reserving the same projector, camera, or test instrument across different days and shifts, with conflict-free scheduling that prevents double bookings automatically.

### 3. Simpler, More Predictable Pricing

EZOfficeInventory’s pricing scales based on item count—250 items at $35/month, 500 items at $50/month, and so on. Features like help desk integrations, SSO, and custom roles are locked behind higher tiers (Premium at $55/month or Enterprise with custom pricing). This means costs can escalate quickly as your inventory grows and your needs mature.

Shelf’s Team plan is $67/month with unlimited items, unlimited users, and the full feature set—custody, bookings, kits, workspaces, and QR operations. No item caps, no per-user fees, no feature gating between tiers for core operational workflows.

See: [Pricing](/pricing)

### 4. Cross-Department Workspaces, Not Just IT

EZOfficeInventory’s DNA is IT service management. Its integrations, workflows, and interface are designed for IT teams managing help desk tickets alongside hardware. Shelf serves the whole organization—IT, media production, facilities, education, and operations—with workspaces that give each department its own inventory view while maintaining organizational oversight. A university can run IT asset tracking and theatre equipment lending from the same account without cross-contamination.

### 5. Kit Management for Multi-Part Equipment

EZOfficeInventory tracks individual assets well—laptops, monitors, keyboards. But production teams, labs, and field operations work with kits: a camera body, three lenses, two batteries, a tripod, and a carrying case that all need to move together. Shelf groups related items into a single bookable unit, tracking every component through the full custody cycle. Return a kit, and Shelf flags what is missing.

### 6. Location Tracking Across Sites

EZOfficeInventory tracks which user an asset is assigned to, but visibility into physical location across buildings, rooms, and job sites can require manual updates. Shelf ties every custody event to a location, giving you a real-time map of where equipment is—not just who has it. For organizations with multiple facilities, warehouses, or job sites, this means less time hunting for gear.

### 7. Open-Source Transparency

EZOfficeInventory is a proprietary SaaS product—you trust the vendor with your data and workflows without visibility into how the software operates. Shelf is open source, meaning teams can audit the code, understand exactly how their data is handled, and contribute to the platform’s development. For organizations with compliance requirements or data sovereignty concerns, this is a meaningful differentiator.

## When Teams Choose Shelf Instead of EZOfficeInventory

Teams often switch to Shelf when EZOfficeInventory’s IT-centric model creates friction outside the help desk:

- **Multi-department organizations**: IT, media, facilities, and operations all need equipment tracking—not just the help desk team. Shelf’s workspaces keep each department independent under one account.
- **Teams with shared equipment pools**: Projectors, cameras, test instruments, and AV gear need booking workflows with conflict prevention, not just assignment records.
- **Growing inventories hitting item caps**: EZOfficeInventory charges more as item counts increase. Shelf’s flat pricing with unlimited items means no cost surprises as the inventory scales.
- **Operations managing kits**: Multi-part equipment sets need to be tracked as units through check-out and return, not as individual line items scattered across a registry.
- **Field teams needing QR-based workflows**: Staff on job sites, warehouse floors, or production sets need to scan and act without logging into a web dashboard.
- **Organizations valuing open-source transparency**: Code visibility, community-driven development, and data auditability matter for compliance and trust.

## When EZOfficeInventory May Be a Better Fit

EZOfficeInventory has genuine strengths for certain use cases:

- **Deep help desk integration**: If your team lives in Zendesk or Jira and needs asset context embedded directly in support tickets, EZOfficeInventory’s native integrations are mature and well-documented.
- **CMMS and preventive maintenance**: Teams focused on scheduling maintenance tasks, tracking repair histories, and managing vendor relationships for device refreshes will find EZOfficeInventory’s maintenance module more developed.
- **IT-only scope**: If your tracking needs are limited to laptops, monitors, and IT peripherals assigned to individual employees, EZOfficeInventory’s assignment model maps directly to that workflow.
- **Enterprise compliance features**: Larger organizations needing SSO, custom security configurations, and private cloud hosting options may find EZOfficeInventory’s Enterprise tier aligned with their requirements.

Both platforms serve asset management needs—the right choice depends on whether your priority is IT service desk integration or fast, cross-department equipment operations.
