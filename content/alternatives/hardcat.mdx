# Hardcat Alternative

Hardcat is an enterprise fixed asset management platform used by government agencies, utilities, and large organizations to manage asset registers, compliance reporting, and financial depreciation. Teams evaluating alternatives often need something faster and more operationally focused—a tool for managing equipment that moves, not just assets that sit on a balance sheet.

---

## Overview: Hardcat vs Shelf

Hardcat approaches asset management from the financial and compliance perspective. It tracks asset values, depreciation schedules, disposal workflows, and regulatory compliance documentation. The platform is typically deployed by finance or asset management teams responsible for maintaining accurate fixed asset registers.

Shelf approaches asset management from the operational perspective. It tracks who has what, when it’s due back, who booked it next, and whether all kit components are accounted for. The platform is used daily by the people who actually handle equipment—staff, technicians, students, and coordinators.

---

## Where Shelf Takes a Different Approach

### 1. Operational Speed vs Administrative Rigor

Hardcat prioritizes compliance and reporting. Every action may require documentation, categorization, and alignment with financial records. Shelf prioritizes speed—scan a QR code, complete the transaction, move on. Audit trails are captured automatically without slowing down the user.

See: [Custody](/features/custody)

---

### 2. Equipment That Moves vs Assets That Sit

Fixed asset management assumes assets are relatively stationary—installed in a building, assigned to a department, depreciated over time. Shelf is built for equipment in constant motion: checked out in the morning, used on location, returned by evening, booked by someone else tomorrow.

See: [Bookings](/features/bookings)

---

### 3. Lightweight Deployment

Hardcat implementations often involve significant IT infrastructure, training programs, and configuration projects. Shelf can be deployed in a day—import your inventory, print QR labels, and start scanning. No server installation, no multi-month implementation.

See: [Asset Tracking](/solutions/asset-tracking)

---

### 4. Kit-Based Workflows

Fixed asset registers track individual items with asset numbers. Shelf tracks kits—grouped equipment that moves together. A mobile broadcast kit, a field testing set, or a classroom technology package is managed as a single operational unit.

See: [Kits](/features/kits)

---

### 5. Open-Source Accessibility

Hardcat is enterprise software with enterprise pricing and closed-source architecture. Shelf is open source, making it accessible to organizations of any size and providing full transparency into how the platform works.

---

## When Teams Choose Shelf Instead of Hardcat

- **Operational teams managing moving equipment**: Gear that changes hands daily needs custody and booking workflows, not asset register entries
- **Organizations wanting fast deployment**: Teams that need to be operational in days, not months
- **Departments with shared equipment pools**: Booking, scheduling, and availability are daily concerns, not annual audit concerns
- **Teams managing kits and multi-part equipment**: Component-level tracking within grouped equipment sets
- **Budget-conscious organizations**: Open-source platform without enterprise licensing costs

---

## When Hardcat May Be a Better Fit

- **Fixed asset register compliance**: Organizations required to maintain formal asset registers for regulatory or financial reporting
- **Government and utility asset management**: Large-scale infrastructure asset tracking with compliance documentation requirements
- **Depreciation and financial lifecycle tracking**: Finance teams managing asset values, write-offs, and disposal processes
