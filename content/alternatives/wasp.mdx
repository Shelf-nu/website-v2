# Wasp Barcode Alternative

Wasp Barcode Technologies provides barcode-based asset tracking solutions that bundle hardware (scanners, printers, mobile computers) with software. The platform has been serving small and mid-sized businesses for decades with a traditional barcode infrastructure approach. Teams exploring alternatives often want to eliminate dedicated hardware requirements and move to smartphone-based workflows with deeper operational features.

---

## Overview: Wasp Barcode vs Shelf

Wasp Barcode takes a hardware-centric approach. Organizations purchase barcode scanners, label printers, and sometimes dedicated mobile computers from Wasp, then use them with Wasp’s desktop or cloud software to track assets. The system is reliable but tied to specific hardware and traditional barcode scanning workflows.

Shelf is hardware-independent. QR labels can be printed on any label printer, and any smartphone camera serves as the scanner. Beyond eliminating hardware purchases, Shelf adds operational depth: booking systems, custody workflows, kit management, and multi-department workspaces that barcode-era platforms were not designed to handle.

---

## Where Shelf Takes a Different Approach

### 1. Smartphone-Native, No Dedicated Hardware

Wasp sells barcode scanners as part of its solution. Shelf works with the phones your team already carries. Every QR scan triggers an operational workflow—check out, return, transfer—without purchasing or maintaining dedicated scanning hardware.

See: [Custody](/features/custody)

---

### 2. QR Codes Over Barcodes

Barcodes store limited data and require line-of-sight scanning with dedicated readers. QR codes are scannable with any phone camera, work at wider angles, and link directly to web-based asset pages where users can take immediate action.

See: [Location Tracking](/features/location-tracking)

---

### 3. Built-In Booking for Shared Equipment

Wasp Barcode focuses on tracking where assets are and who has them. Shelf adds a scheduling layer: teams book equipment in advance, view real-time availability, and avoid double-bookings—capabilities that barcode-based tracking platforms were not designed to include.

See: [Bookings](/features/bookings)

---

### 4. Kit and Accessory Management

Barcode systems track individual items in flat lists. Shelf groups related items into kits with parent-child relationships. Book a presentation kit, and the projector, laptop, clicker, and cables are all tracked together—checked out together, verified together on return.

See: [Kits](/features/kits)

---

### 5. Cloud-Native and Open Source

Wasp offers both on-premise and cloud options, but the architecture reflects its on-premise roots. Shelf is cloud-native and open source—no servers to maintain, no software to install, and full code transparency for teams that want to inspect or self-host.

---

## When Teams Choose Shelf Instead of Wasp Barcode

- **Teams eliminating hardware costs**: No barcode scanners, no dedicated mobile computers—just smartphones and printed QR labels
- **Organizations needing booking workflows**: Shared equipment environments require reservation and scheduling features
- **Operations managing kits**: Multi-component equipment needs grouped tracking, not individual barcode scans
- **Multi-department organizations**: Workspace separation gives each team its own inventory without multiple software licenses
- **Teams wanting modern cloud workflows**: Browser-based, mobile-friendly, and accessible from anywhere without VPN or desktop software

---

## When Wasp Barcode May Be a Better Fit

- **Organizations with existing Wasp hardware**: Teams already invested in Wasp scanners and infrastructure
- **On-premise requirements**: Organizations that cannot use cloud-hosted solutions due to policy or connectivity constraints
- **High-speed warehouse scanning**: Dedicated barcode hardware scans faster than phone cameras in high-throughput warehouse environments
