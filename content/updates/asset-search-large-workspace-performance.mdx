If you have a large inventory and the search bar at the top of your asset list had started feeling slow — or in the worst cases timing out — that's fixed.

A customer with around 13,000 assets was seeing simple searches take 30 to 60 seconds. The cause was twofold: substring matches like `ILIKE '%term%'` against `sequentialId`, barcode value, QR id, and category/location/tag names had no trigram index to lean on, so the database fell back to scanning entire tables; and every row returned by the asset list was eagerly loading its custom-field values, even though the simple asset index never displays them.

This release adds GIN trigram indexes on the columns the simple search bar actually scans — `Asset.sequentialId`, `Category.name`, `Location.name`, `Tag.name`, `Qr.id`, and `Barcode.value` — and stops the eager custom-field load on the simple list. There is also a new fast path: when every search term looks like an asset identifier (a bare number like `21035`, a UPC barcode, or a canonical sequential ID like `SAM-0001`), the query narrows to just the three columns where an ID-shaped value can legitimately live — sequential ID, barcode value, and QR id — instead of fanning out across ten different OR branches.

The fix applies automatically. Searches that look like asset IDs feel instant; substring searches across category, location, and tag names benefit from the new indexes too. The command palette and the advanced asset index continue to search the full set of fields, including custom-field values.
