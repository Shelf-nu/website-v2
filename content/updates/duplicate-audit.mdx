If you run audits on a recurring cadence — monthly stock counts, post-event sweeps, quarterly compliance checks — you can now re-run an audit on the same asset list in one click instead of rebuilding the scope each time.

**How it works**: open any audit that has reached **Completed**, **Cancelled**, or **Archived** status, click the actions menu (⋯), and choose **Duplicate audit**. Shelf creates a new Pending session that inherits the source audit's name (with a "(Copy)" suffix), description, and asset list. Notes, scans, assignee, and due date do **not** carry over — the duplicate starts clean so it doesn't muddy the new round's results.

**If the source audit's assets have shifted**, Shelf is up-front about it. A yellow warning shows the dropped count if some of the original assets have been deleted from your workspace since the source audit ran (e.g. *"3 of 19 original assets are no longer in your workspace and will be dropped"*); the duplicate still proceeds with the remaining assets. If every original asset is gone, the dialog blocks duplication and prompts you to create a new audit from scratch — no silent failures.

Duplicate is available to **Admin** and **Owner** roles on terminal-state audits only. Pending and Active audits keep their existing **Edit** and **Cancel** actions.
