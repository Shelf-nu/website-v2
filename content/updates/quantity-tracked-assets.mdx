Shelf has always tracked every asset as a single, uniquely-identifiable item — perfect for laptops, cameras, and vehicles, but a poor fit for supplies you manage by count. You can now choose how each asset is tracked.

When you add or edit an asset, pick a **tracking method**: **Individually tracked** (one record per item, exactly as before) or **Tracked by quantity** (one record for a pool of identical units). Quantity-tracked assets add a **quantity**, a **unit of measure** (pcs, boxes, liters), an optional **min quantity** low-stock threshold, and a **consumption type** — "Used up" for disposables that are consumed when handed out, or "Returnable" for reusable supplies that come back with a report of what returned versus what was lost.

Quantity changes flow through the rest of Shelf: custody becomes quantity-aware (several people can each hold a portion of the same pool), bookings can reserve a quantity from available stock, restocking increases the count when new shipments arrive, and every change is logged for an audit trail. Set a min quantity and the workspace owner gets an email when stock runs low, so you can reorder before you run out.

Learn how to set it up in [Quantity-Tracked Assets and Consumables](/knowledge-base/quantity-tracked-assets-and-consumables).
